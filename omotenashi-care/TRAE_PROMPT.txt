 ## Project tasks 
  
 # MediVoice — AI Medical Communication App 
  
 # Hackathon Build Prompt for TRAE 
  
 ## PROBLEM 
  
 Patients — especially foreigners — face critical communication barriers in healthcare: 
  
 - Medical terminology is too complex for patients to understand 
 - Foreign patients without interpreters are often rejected by doctors 
 - Human interpreters translate words but miss emotional sentiment and nuance 
 - Patients cannot accurately describe symptoms in a foreign language 
 - Misunderstandings between doctors, nurses, and patients lead to misdiagnosis 
 - Even diagnostic tests (blood work, consultations) fail when patient history is incomplete 
  
 Real context: Japan has 3.5M+ foreign residents. Thai, Vietnamese, Chinese, Korean patients 
 regularly face rejection or misdiagnosis due to language barriers. 
  
 --- 
  
 ## OBJECTIVE 
  
 Build a real-time AI-powered medical communication bridge called **MediVoice** that: 
  
 1. Lets patients speak or type symptoms in their native language 
 2. Translates AND preserves emotional sentiment (not just words) 
 3. Generates a structured clinical summary for the doctor in Japanese 
 4. Translates the doctor's response back to the patient's language 
 5. Removes the need for human interpreters in basic consultations 
  
 --- 
  
 ## SOLUTION OVERVIEW 
  
 Two-panel web app (Patient view + Doctor view) with an AI layer in between. 
  
 Core flow: 
 Patient speaks (any language) 
 → Speech-to-text transcription 
 → AI: translate + extract symptoms + detect sentiment + write clinical note 
 → Doctor sees structured summary card in Japanese 
 → Doctor responds in Japanese/English 
 → AI translates response back to patient language 
 → Patient hears/reads response in their language 
  
 Key differentiator: We preserve SENTIMENT, not just words. 
 A patient saying "I feel heavy here" in Thai gets tagged as [anxious + cardiac risk] 
 — not just translated as "chest discomfort."

 HACKATHON BUILD PROMPT 
 App: Omotenashi Care (おもてなしケア) 
 Track: ⛩️ Omotenashi AI 
 Stack: TRAE + MiniMax + Agora 
  
 ## PROBLEM 
  
 Every day in Japan, foreign patients walk into hospitals scared and in pain 
 — and get turned away or misdiagnosed because of language barriers. 
  
 Japan has 3.5 million+ foreign residents plus 35 million tourists per year. 
 Thai, Vietnamese, Chinese, Korean, Indonesian patients face: 
  
 1. No interpreter available → doctor refuses to treat 
 2. Human interpreters translate WORDS not FEELINGS 
 → A Thai patient saying "I feel something pressing here" becomes 
 "chest pain" in Japanese. The emotional urgency is lost. 
 3. Patients cannot describe symptoms accurately in Japanese 
 4. Doctors miss critical context → misdiagnosis 
 5. Foreign patients feel scared, dismissed, and alone 
  
 This is the opposite of Omotenashi — Japan's philosophy of wholehearted, 
 anticipatory hospitality. 
  
 ## OBJECTIVE 
  
 Build "Omotenashi Care" — an AI medical communication bridge that: 
  
 1. Lets patients speak/type symptoms in their native language 
 2. Translates AND preserves emotional sentiment (not just words) 
 3. Generates a structured clinical note for the doctor in Japanese 
 4. Translates the doctor's response warmly back to the patient 
 5. (Phase 2) Speaks the response aloud via MiniMax TTS through Agora 
  
 ## SOLUTION 
  
 Two-panel Streamlit web app: Patient (left) + Doctor (right) 
  
 Core flow: 
 Patient speaks/types in their language 
 ↓ 
 [Agora] captures audio stream (Phase 2) 
 ↓ 
 [MiniMax STT] speech → text (Phase 2) 
 ↓ 
 [MiniMax LLM] → translate + symptoms + sentiment + clinical note 
 ↓ 
 Doctor sees structured Japanese summary card 
 ↓ 
 Doctor types/speaks response (JP or EN) 
 ↓ 
 [MiniMax LLM] → translate to patient language 
 ↓ 
 [MiniMax TTS] → warm voice in patient language (Phase 2) 
 ↓ 
 [Agora] delivers audio to patient (Phase 2)

 ## SOFTWARE REQUIREMENTS 
  
 ### Functional 
  
 FR1  Patient selects language from: Thai, Vietnamese, Chinese, Korean, 
 English, Indonesian, Tagalog, Hindi, Arabic, Spanish 
 FR2  Patient inputs symptoms via text area (Phase 1) / voice (Phase 2) 
 FR3  On "Send to Doctor": system returns within 3 seconds: 
 - Japanese translation of patient's message 
 - List of detected symptoms (as tag pills) 
 - Sentiment: anxious | calm | distressed | confused 
 - Urgency: low | medium | high 
 - Clinical note in Japanese 
 FR4  Doctor sees all above in a structured summary card 
 FR5  Doctor types response in Japanese or English 
 FR6  System translates and delivers response to patient in their language 
 FR7  (Phase 2) MiniMax TTS speaks doctor response warmly in patient language 
 FR8  (Phase 2) Agora connects patient and doctor voice/video 
  
 ### Non-Functional 
  
 NFR1  App runs in mock/demo mode with NO API keys (for testing) 
 NFR2  Two-panel responsive layout 
 NFR3  Session state managed via Streamlit st.session_state 
 NFR4  All API calls wrapped in try/except with graceful fallback to mock
